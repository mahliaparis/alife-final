The fitness curve shows two main values across generations: best fitness in the generation 
and average fitness of the population. Both values generally increase over time, which 
indicates that the GA is improving the ants’ foraging behavior. In the early generations, 
fitness is relatively low because the genomes are randomly initialized. This means the ants 
mostly move randomly and rarely form consistent pheromone trails. As evolution progresses, 
parameter combinations that improve pheromone-following and nest-return behavior become more 
common, leading to higher fitness values.

The average fitness represents how the population as a whole is improving. In the first few 
generations, most genomes perform poorly, so the average fitness stays low. Over time, 
successful traits spread through the population through crossover and mutation. As more genomes 
begin to produce effective behaviors, the average fitness gradually increases.

The best fitness in each generation represents the highest-performing genome in that generation. 
This value often increases more quickly than the average fitness because even a single genome 
with a strong parameter combination can produce much better performance than the rest of the 
population. This can appear as sudden increases in the best fitness line when the GA discovers 
a genome that produces stronger pheromone trails or more efficient food retrieval.

The code also keeps track of a global best genome, which is the highest fitness individual 
discovered during the entire run of the simulation. This genome is saved and replayed at the 
end so the final visualization shows the most effective behavior evolved by the GA. While 
the best genome in a generation may fluctuate, the global best represents the strongest 
solution discovered overall.

Several implementation choices contribute to the fitness improvements seen in the graph. The 
fitness function mainly rewards the amount of food returned to the nest, with a small additional 
reward for overall pheromone trail strength. This encourages behaviors that both collect food 
efficiently and reinforce trails that other ants can follow. The mutation and crossover operations 
allow the GA to explore new combinations of behavior parameters, while elite selection ensures 
that the best-performing genomes are preserved between generations. Together, these mechanisms 
gradually improve the colony’s behavior and produce the upward trend seen in the fitness curve, 
which is similar to how beneficial traits spread through populations in biological evolution.
